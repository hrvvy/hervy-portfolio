# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, founders, and marketing peers evaluating Hervy Lee (commercial growth strategist, Singapore). They arrive from a resume link or a referral, usually skim-first, and decide within a minute whether the depth is worth reading. Secondary: prospective clients doing due diligence.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that proves one thesis: most growth problems are commercial problems disguised as marketing problems. It converts skepticism into a conversation by showing receipts: five case studies with measured numbers, an operating sequence applied per engagement, and in-production internal AI tools. Success = the reader emails.

## Brand Personality

Evidence-led, dry-witted, premium. The voice is "the friend who did their homework": confident claims, numbers labelled honestly (measured vs modelled), small sarcasm, no hype. Emotionally: trust first, quiet delight second.

## Anti-references

- Agency pitch sites ("we take on a small number of engagements" energy); the site must read as a person, not a vendor.
- Tacky direct-response aesthetics: countdown timers, neon CTAs, testimonials walls.
- AI-slop landing pages: generic hero metrics, identical card grids, stock-photo teams, em-dash-riddled copy.

## Design Principles

- Evidence over claims: every number traceable to a dashboard, account history, or an explicitly labelled model. The design's job is to make proof legible.
- Typography carries the brand: dark editorial ground, system serif for statements, restrained steel-blue accent. No decoration that a number or a sentence could do better.
- One animated moment per page, earned: the "growth" hero choreography is the signature; everything else stays quiet.
- The thesis organizes everything: case pages follow situation, diagnosis, sequence, moves, numbers. Structure is the argument.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast targets on both light and dark themes; full prefers-reduced-motion alternatives for every animation (hero choreography, reveals, scroll cue); keyboard-accessible nav dropdown with visible focus states; semantic headings and aria labels on decorative elements.
