Case study · Healthcare / beauty · 2024–present

Organic trust is an asset. It is not an acquisition system.

BeFound had built something genuinely rare: a beauty business growing purely through Instagram content and workshops, with real customer trust and zero paid acquisition. The ceiling was just as real. Growth moved at the speed of the founder's content calendar, and a ~S$3.5K programme deserved a pipeline that did not depend on the algorithm's mood.

Engagement: Business Elevates Visit the Skin Barrier page ↗

The situation

When we met in 2024, BeFound had never spent a meaningful dollar on ads. Everything came through organic Instagram reach and in-person workshops. That is a wonderful proof of product and a terrible growth plan: every month restarted from zero, and the founder was the bottleneck for every new customer.

Going from organic-only to paid is where premium brands usually hurt themselves. Cold traffic does not arrive pre-sold by months of content, so brands respond by discounting, which quietly repositions them as cheap. The challenge was to build a first acquisition engine that could sell a ~S$3.5K programme to strangers without spending the brand's premium to do it.

The diagnosis

BeFound did not have a traffic problem; Meta sells traffic to anyone. It had a translation problem: the trust that organic content built over months had to be rebuilt in minutes for a cold audience. That takes a position the market can immediately understand and a mechanism that lets a stranger diagnose themselves into the brand's world.

The market gap was clear. Everyone in beauty sells treatments; almost nobody owns the underlying category of why skin misbehaves. Skin Barrier Health became that ground: specific enough to be credible, broad enough to hold a full customer journey.

Where the work concentrated

The full sequence, with this engagement's heaviest stages highlighted
Economics→ Market→ Customer→ Positioning→ Offer→ Landing page→ Creative→ Campaigns→ Measurement→ Systems

A first paid engine is mostly positioning and funnel work. The programme itself was already strong; the job was making it legible to people who had never heard of the brand.

What we built

1

Positioned the company around Skin Barrier Health

One idea the brand could own outright: clear, calm skin comes down to the health of the skin barrier. Every ad, page, and consult now reasons from the same premise, which is what makes a brand feel coherent to a stranger.

2

Built a quiz funnel as the front door

Nobody books a ~S$3.5K consultation straight off an ad. A skin barrier quiz lets prospects diagnose their own situation first, converting curiosity into a qualified, context-rich lead before any human conversation happens.

3

Redesigned the landing pages

The pages were rebuilt to look the way the programme is priced: calm, warm, premium. A S$3.5K programme cannot be sold off a page that looks like a flash sale.

4

Stood up the first paid acquisition engine

A full appointment-to-close funnel: campaigns feeding the quiz, the quiz feeding consultations, consultations feeding a ~S$3.5K core programme, all measured in a live dashboard so the economics stay visible month to month.

The numbers

S$220K+ ad spend managed
S$3M+ customer lifetime value generated
S$300–570 CAC against a S$3.9–5.8K blended AOV
9–13× monthly ROAS
S$50–80K typical monthly revenue from the funnel; peak months exceed S$100K

Monthly ranges, 2024 through present; core programme priced from ~S$3.5K.

The funnel gave an organic brand a pipeline, without spending its premium to get one.
