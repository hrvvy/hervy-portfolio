Case study · Education · 2023–2026

Build an offer competitors cannot match. Then build the machine that scales it.

The Edu Experience was one learning centre in Tampines competing in Singapore's most crowded service market: tuition. Three years later it operates four centres across the island. The expansion was built on two things, in strict order: a commercial model worth copying, and an acquisition system that made each new location viable from opening day.

Engagement: Business Elevates Visit the Unlimited Tuition page ↗

The situation

Tuition in Singapore is a knife fight. Every centre sells the same subjects, the same grade promises, and the same per-hour pricing structure, which means parents comparison-shop on price and proximity. A single centre in that market can survive on local reputation. It cannot expand on it.

The diagnosis

Per-hour pricing is the commodity trap of the entire industry. It frames tuition as buying hours, invites direct price comparison, and punishes the students who need the most help, because they are the ones who need the most hours. Break that frame and the comparison stops working. Working closely with the founder, that became the Unlimited Tuition model: a commercial structure parents cannot line up against an hourly rate, and one that aligns the centre with outcomes instead of hours sold.

A differentiated model solves being chosen. Expansion needs something else: acquisition that works in each new neighbourhood, not just the one where reputation already lives.

Where the work concentrated

The full sequence, with this engagement's heaviest stages highlighted
Economics→ Market→ Customer→ Positioning→ Offer→ Landing page→ Creative→ Campaigns→ Measurement→ Systems

The offer did the differentiating; the campaign architecture did the scaling. Neither works alone: a copyable offer scales competitors, and a great offer without distribution stays a local secret.

What we built

1

Created the Unlimited Tuition commercial model

Developed with the founder as the centre's core differentiation: parents buy outcomes and access, not hours. It removed the industry's standard price comparison and became the reason to choose the centre over the one next door.

2

Built location-level acquisition systems

Each centre gets its own campaign infrastructure tuned to its catchment area, so a new location opens with a working enquiry engine rather than waiting years for word of mouth to accumulate.

3

Ran the engine through the expansion

185+ campaigns and S$260K+ in managed spend across the growth from one centre to four, holding a blended cost per lead of about S$27 in a market where undifferentiated centres pay multiples of that.

The offer made the centre worth choosing. The system made it worth four locations.
